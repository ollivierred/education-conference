Rationale

Empower Higher Education, Establish Public Private Partnership

Higher Education has played an important role in the process of joining to the global processes and integrating to the global production network. Shift to the knowledge-based economy has increased the need for the highly qualified labor, innovations, and research. At the same time, recent global financial crisis hit all economies with varying degree. This resulted in the decline of public spending on education in general, and especially, on higher education. As a result of the pressured public budgeting, the higher education institutions faced the challenges of maintaining/improving quality of learning and research, and meeting the increased demand for access to higher education with fewer resources.

The goal of Education Conference 2017

Education Conference 2017, aims to increase empower delegates from four different areas: policy makers, researchers, NGO’s, entrepreneurs and private businesses to discuss alternative ways of income generation through establishing public private partnership, alumni engagement, and translational universities.

Its panel discussion will involve three representatives: policy makers, researchers, and entrepreneurs. The panelists will address the findings presented in the research papers presented during the exhibition.

Its four main panels and workshops cover the hot topics that are mostly debated on the policy and institution levels as in the Caucasus (especially Georgia and Azerbaijan) and in the EU member states (especially Eastern Europe).

Workshops and Descriptions

  1. Risk management in establishment of direct business-university contracts

    Preparation: Academics and business representatives to develop their proposals (business offers and academic offers) for particular business-university contracts. Defining Risk and Risk management methods: some basic theory to be presented as "instructions".-U2B face-to-face negotiations: each participant should have at least 2-4 meetings with other representatives to match their offers. -Results: some of the participants will be invited to present the offers, approved from the academics and business representatives. Outcomes: academics to feel that "be entrepreneurial brings more opportunities for development, than just to wait".

  2. Entrepreneurial approach in Higher Education through internationalisation and transnational education (TNE)

    As the levels of financial support for universities decrease around the world, there is an increasing pressure for institutions to become more entrepreneurial and forward looking. This coupled with growing international pressure and competition from other providers has led to a number of flexible opportunities using internationalisation as the main drive.

    Internationalisation continues to be on the agenda of many education providers worldwide. It has significance for the contribution that HE makes to the development of a nation, its people; and its ability to compete in a global market. It is integral to economic well-being, driven and enabled by liberalisation of the international trade.

    Internationalisation and transnational education in higher education are life changing and allow for better quality and diversity of academic programmes. Despite its growing importance, for many HE practitioners these are still messy concepts. They are variously interpreted and they often build upon narrow preconceptions limited to international student recruitment and foreign fees.

    This interactive workshop is looking at different ways of creating “cross-border” access to international education through various means, such as international recruitment, foreign campuses, student and staff mobility, progression arrangements, on-line provision, Summer Schools, work placements, franchised arrangements and validations, to name just a few. It refers to a number of examples of good practice from Coventry University in the UK, which has been actively expanding its transnational education activities for many years. Through a number of activities, we will create a discussion about the role of internationalisation and consider different transnational opportunities, asses their level of risk, advantages and disadvantages.

  3. Law and practice of higher education infrastructure PPP projects in the European Union, particular example of PPP projects in Hungary. He is specialized in European Union law, projects and finance and intellectual property law.

    Higher education is a key sector for the use of PPP in Europe and there are increasing number of PPP projects being launched. The workshop aims to present and assess the current role of public private partnerships in delivering public services in the higher education sector and their potential future role in doing so. It will address economic, financial, operational and legal considerations and will provide with a practical overview of the legal framework and contractual structures applied in Europe for PPP projects. The objective of the workshop is to provide a practical experience in the process of planning and procuring PPP in the higher education sector. The workshop will include a presentation and discussion of experience of PPP projects. The style of the workshop will be interactive to ensure the widest possible exchange of experiences and perspectives.

  4. Design Thinking/ Entrepreneurial Spirit and Sustainable Development Goals / Mindset. Introduction to Design Thinking (DT)/ Entrepreneurial Spirit (ES) in new programmes/ modules/ services development

    The program will introduce the idea of Design Thinking, Mootee (2013) that ‘everything has changed, is changing, and will continue to change’. The 3-hour programme is to provide insights to participants about the growing trend of integrating sustainable development mindset/ goals with ISO 9001, 26000 CSR Guidelines and ISO 10015 Training Guidelines for the future.

      Course Outline:

    1. Introduction to Design Thinking and Entrepreneurial Spirit (2 hrs)

      Concept of Design Thinking and Entrepreneurial Spirit Application and Case study

    2. Linking IS0 9000, 26000, 10015 for Sustainable Development (2 hrs)

      Main clauses of ISO 9001 and 10015 Application of the clauses into management system for continual improvement and sustainable development.

Research Gallery and Facutly Showcase

Research Gallery: will enable the young researchers and faculties to present their scientific findings to the audience. The participants (policy makers, industry representatives, NGO, researchers, university administration) will be able to approach the researchers and ask questions and discuss their findings on the spot. The presenters will use posters.

Faculty showcase is one of the formats of the presentations offered to the applicants of the Education Conference. Faculty showcase enables faculty researchers to present their research outcomes they conducted with their students. The research team including faculty researchers and supervised students present their collaborative work at the conference. The faculty showcase will take place with the research gallery.
